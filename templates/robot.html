01 — What Is Robotics

THE INTERSECTION
OF SCIENCE &
ENGINEERING

Robotics is where science, engineering and technology converge to produce machines that substitute for — or replicate — human actions. From R2-D2 to Boston Dynamics' Atlas, the field has expanded far beyond imagination.


In 2005, 90% of all robots assembled cars. Today, they explore Earth's harshest conditions, assist law enforcement, and operate in nearly every facet of healthcare.


01

Mechanical Construction

Every robot has a physical form designed for its specific environment — from titanium Mars rover wheels to micro-scale surgical instruments.

02

Electrical Components

An electric current powers the machinery — batteries, capacitors, or external power supplies enabling motion and computation.

03

Computer Programming

Code transforms metal into intelligence — telling the robot when, how, and why to carry out its assigned task.

02 — Classification

FIVE TYPES
OF ROBOTS

Type 01

Pre-Programmed

Operate in controlled environments performing simple, monotonous tasks — like welding a car door on an assembly line faster than any human.

Type 02

Humanoid

Mimic human behavior — running, jumping, carrying objects. Examples include Sophia by Hanson Robotics and Boston Dynamics' Atlas.

Type 03

Autonomous

Operate independently in open environments without human supervision — like the Roomba vacuum navigating your home via sensors.

Type 04

Teleoperated

Human-controlled bots for extreme conditions — from deep-sea pipe repair to landmine-detecting drones on active battlefields.

Type 05

Augmenting

Enhance or restore human capabilities — robotic prosthetic limbs, exoskeletons for heavy lifting, and surgical precision tools.

03 — Industry Use

WHERE ROBOTS
WORK TODAY

⚙️

Manufacturing

The oldest adopter of robotics. Co-bots work alongside humans to test and assemble cars, electronics, and industrial equipment — with over 3 million units active globally.

📦

Logistics

Robots pick, pack and ship your orders. Last-mile delivery bots now bring packages directly to your door, while warehouse robots cut fulfillment time to minutes.

🏥

Healthcare

From robot-assisted surgery to autonomous hospital delivery bots like TUG, and Toyota's rehab assistants that help patients learn to walk again.

🚗

Travel

Self-driving vehicles from Tesla, Waymo and Ford are redefining transportation. Uber and Lyft are building autonomous fleets — no human driver required.

🏠

Home

Robots vacuum your floors, mow your lawn, clean your pool, and entertain your kids. Science fiction arrived quietly through the front door.

🌾

Agriculture

Harvesting robots, drone-based crop monitoring, and autonomous tractors are powering the farmtech revolution — growing more with less.
